# Wellstar demo — Denial recovery on the governed platform

**The one problem this demo solves:** appealable denied-claims revenue written
off because appeal windows lapse before anyone works the denial — with every
AI step governed, human-approved, and auditable.

Audience: a physician + an administrator. Duration: ~10 minutes.
Everything shown is the real platform — real services, real authorization,
real four-eyes governance. The data is synthetic and fully fictional
(fictional payers, fake NPIs, no PHI), uploaded as tenant data.

## The demo arc (click path + talk track)

**1. The money picture — login `admin@wellstar-demo` → Dashboards.**
Three live dashboards (RCM Command Center, Denial Analytics, A/R Aging
Actions), every chart on real (synthetic) data: denied dollars by category
and payer, overturn history, A/R aging. *Talk track (admin): "This is your
835 remittance feed landing as governed data — the same numbers your RCM
team reconciles by hand, live and drillable."*

**2. The worklist — Cases ("Denials & A/R Worklist").**
Nine open denials, each titled in domain language with payer, CARC code,
denied amount, and — the beat — **days left on the appeal window**. Open
**DN-3001**: $18,450 oncology chemo administration, CO-197 (auth absent),
**12 days left, not appealed**. *Talk track: "This is the claim that dies
quietly today. Twelve days from now it's a write-off."*

**3. The rules fire — Decisions → "Denial worklist triage" → evaluate DN-3001
(dry-run first).**
Rule #0 fires: appeal window nearly closed + not appealed → **CRITICAL,
escalate**. Show the rule trace — plain-English, deterministic, and the
thresholds are the *institution's own configuration*, not a black box.
*Physician beat:* scroll to the **coverage rule** — LCD/NCD medical-necessity
denials are NEVER auto-routed to appeal; the table's own note says clinical
review decides. *"The system knows what it's not allowed to decide."*

**4. Governed proposal → four-eyes approval.**
Run evaluate for real: a governed proposal is created (not an action — a
proposal). Switch to `director@wellstar-demo` → Approvals inbox → the
proposal shows the full rationale + predicted effect. Approve. *Talk track:
"A named human approved this. Try approving your own proposal — the platform
blocks it. Every consequential action needs a second person."*

**5. The receipt.**
Back on the case: resolved, disposition applied, the resolution note carries
the exact rule trace. Audit trail shows the whole chain — who proposed, who
approved, what executed, in an immutable log (exportable to their SIEM).
*Close (admin): "Denied → worked → recovered, with proof. The dashboard's
overturned-appeals number is the money slide."*

## Q&A cheat sheet (honest answers only)

- **"Is this using our data?"** No — fully synthetic, fictional payers/NPIs,
  generated by `deploy/demo/wellstar_rcm_data.py`. Your real data would come
  in over the pack's governed connectors (X12 835 SFTP drop is a built-in,
  decoded natively) or file upload, into your own RLS-isolated tenant.
- **"Can the AI deny/appeal on its own?"** No, architecturally: every write
  is a proposal; execution requires a signed human-approval grant, a
  per-resource authorization on the exact case, and per-tenant tool
  enablement — all enforced server-side and audited, not UI conventions.
- **"What about PHI?"** Per-tenant row-level isolation, agent guardrails
  with PII redaction on model egress, BYO SSO/IdP, immutable audit + SIEM
  export. (Demo data contains no PHI at all.)
- **"What do physicians get?"** Fewer denial fire-drills: administrative and
  documentation defects are routed and drafted before they reach a
  clinician; only genuinely clinical calls (coverage/medical-necessity)
  escalate for physician review — with the record assembled.
- **Numbers discipline:** keep industry stats qualitative ("a large share of
  appealable denials are never worked") — the demo's own recovered-vs-denied
  dashboard is the number that matters in the room.

## Known rough edges (don't discover these live)

- Dev login is the local auth mode; a prospect-facing hosted demo would use
  the real OIDC/SSO path (built, but needs an IdP configured).
- The agent copilot narrative (LLM reasoning over case evidence) runs on
  local Ollama — latency is minutes-not-seconds on a laptop; the decision-
  table + four-eyes arc above needs no LLM and is instant. Lead with it.
- A resolved spare stays resolved — count your rehearsals (3 hero-class
  cases per build).
